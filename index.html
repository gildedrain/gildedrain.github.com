Here's my approach.

Start with a sketch.

A quick sketch is the fastest way to remove the fog of confusion and pave the way for effective problem-solving — whether on a whiteboard, on a napkin, or in the dirt.

  • Gather the requirements.
  • Help the team to see the problem visually.
  • Show where we are and where we're going.
  • Show how it's all interconnected.
  • Guide the brainstorm.

Swim around a bit.

Learn all there is to know about the problem and the spaces surrounding it. A little extra time here can save a bundle down the line.

  • Zoom out and look at the bigger picture.
  • Map the user's journey.
  • Think of things the client didn't.
  • Make sure we all understand what problem we're working to solve.

Design with intent.

Keep the user in mind throughout the design process and remained focused on the problem we're working to solve.

  • Simplify & Streamline.
  • Plan for the worst-case scenarios.
  • Leverage the systems already in place.
  • Make it pretty.
  • Get more eyes on it.

Build it together.

Every project is a team effort. Encourage teamwork and clarity by translating complicated problems between managers, designers, engineers and customers.

  • Make it work – for every user on every device.
  • Make it reusable – for us.
  • Test it with the client as soon as possible.
  • Launch it!
  • Ask what went right & wrong.
