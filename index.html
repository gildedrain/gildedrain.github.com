Here's my approach.

It always starts with a sketch.

A quick sketch is the fastest way to remove the fog of confusion and pave the way for effective problem-solving — whether on a whiteboard, on a napkin, or in the dirt.

  • Get the team to see the problem visually.
  • Guide the collaborative effort.
  • Give context to changes in the process.
  • Show how it's all interconnected.

Swim in it.

Learn all there is to know about the problem and the spaces surrounding it.

  • Gather the requirements.
  • Zoom out and look at the bigger picture.
  • Map the user's journey.
  • Think of things the customer didn't.
  • Make sure we all understand what problem we're working to solve.

Design with purpose.

Turn the music up and get into a flow. This is where I long to be.

  • Simplify.
  • Streamline.
  • Solve the problem.
  • Plan for the worst-case scenarios.
  • Make it pretty.
  • Get more eyes on it.

Build it together.

Every project is a team effort. Encourage teamwork and clarity by translating complicated problems between managers, designers, engineers and customers.

  • Make it accessible.
  • Make it responsive and browser agnostic.
  • Make it reusable.
  • Test it with the customer as soon as possible.
  • Launch it!
